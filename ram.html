Life Summary

Vaijanath

0-5 Years

Birth to 12 months Rapid physical growth: gains in height, weight, and head size. Learns to roll, sit, crawl, and sometimes walk by the end of the first year. Begins to recognize faces, respond to sounds, and smile socially. Walks steadily, starts running, and climbs furniture. Learns to feed self with hands or a spoon. Vocabulary expands rapidly (often 50+ words by age 2).Runs, jumps, and kicks a ball. Begins potty training. Improved coordination: can ride a tricycle, throw/catch a ball. Speaks in full sentences and asks many questions (“why?”). Understands concepts like big/small, colors, and some numbers. Engages in more complex imaginative play.Speaks clearly and can tell stories. Understands rules and starts following routines. Starts developing friendships and empathy. .

6-10 Years

earns to read basic words, write letters, and count higher. Improved motor skills: cuts with scissors, draws shapes, begins sports. Reads fluently, begins writing paragraphs and stories. Understands more complex math and logic (multiplication, division). Becomes more self-aware and sensitive to criticism. Increased physical strength and coordination. More responsible and capable of setting short-term goals. Understands abstract ideas like time, money, and values. Begins to form identity: what they like, believe, and who they are. Prepares emotionally for adolescence; may become moody or private. .

11-15 Years

Puberty begins (earlier in girls): physical changes like growth spurts, body hair, voice change. Becomes more self-conscious and aware of appearance. Experiences stronger emotions and mood swings. Physical development continues (more pronounced in boys now). Learns to manage complex emotions better (though not always consistently). Begins thinking about the future: career interests, values, life goals. Forms deeper emotional connections; values loyalty in friendships

16-20 Years

Gains greater independence — often starts driving or working part-time. More emotionally mature, but still prone to mood swings or stress. Forms strong peer relationships; friendships may feel like chosen family. Self-identity becomes more defined: “Who am I?” starts getting clearer. Career or education path becomes a central focus. Emotional regulation and self-discipline continue to improve. Forms deeper, more mature romantic and social relationships.
